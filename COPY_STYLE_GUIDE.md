# MyFoodFit Copy Style Guide (Locked)

## Brand Voice
- Neutral
- Informational
- Calm
- Non-judgmental
- Supportive but not motivational
- Not a coach
- Not medical advice
- Not aspirational or emotional

Short examples:
- "Here is a clear summary of the product details."
- "This is an informational view of the label."

## What MyFoodFit Is
- A food decision-support tool
- Explains food information clearly
- Helps users make informed choices
- Facts first, interpretation second

Short examples:
- "Based on the information available, this product is higher in sodium."
- "Facts first, interpretation second."

## What MyFoodFit Is Not
- Not a personal nutrition coach
- Not a transformation program
- Not motivational
- Not lifestyle branding
- Not medical guidance

Short examples:
- "We do not provide medical guidance."
- "This tool supports informed choices, not transformations."

## Language Rules
Allowed phrases:
- "Based on the information available..."
- "This product is higher in..."
- "May be less suitable if..."
- "Fits your selected preferences"

Disallowed phrases:
- "Your personal coach"
- "Take control of your health"
- "Transform"
- "Optimise"
- "Feel better"
- "We guide you"
- "Perfect for you"

## RAG Explanations
- Red / Amber / Green are informational signals only
- Never imply danger, guilt, or instruction
- No commands like "avoid", "must", or "should"

Short examples:
- "Red indicates a higher level of this nutrient."
- "Amber indicates a moderate level."
- "Green indicates a lower level."

## Tone Checklist (Mandatory)
Every screen and page must pass:
- Would this sound OK in a medical waiting room?
- Does it explain rather than persuade?
- Is the user free to decide?

Short examples:
- "This information is provided to help you decide."
- "You are free to choose what fits your needs."

## Cursor Enforcement Note
Any future copy that violates this guide is a bug, not a creative choice.
